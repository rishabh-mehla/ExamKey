ExamKey — Privacy Policy
Last updated: March 25, 2026

ExamKey ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use the ExamKey mobile application ("App").

1. Information We Collect
1.1 Information You Provide
Phone Number: If you choose to sign in via OTP, we collect your phone number for authentication purposes via Firebase Authentication.
Exam Responses: The answers you mark for exam questions are stored on your device and optionally synced to our cloud service to compute scores.
Exam Preferences: Which exams you are preparing for (e.g., JEE Main, NEET).
1.2 Information Collected Automatically
Device Information: Device model, operating system version, and app version for crash reporting and analytics.
Usage Data: How you interact with the App (screens viewed, features used) via Firebase Analytics.
Crash Reports: Diagnostic data when the app crashes, collected via Firebase Crashlytics.
1.3 Information from Third Parties
Advertising: Google AdMob may collect device identifiers and usage data to serve personalized ads. See Google's Privacy Policy.
2. How We Use Your Information
To compute and display your exam scores against multiple answer key sources
To provide rank predictions and performance analytics
To send push notifications about new answer keys and score updates
To improve the App and fix bugs
To display advertisements (for free tier users)
To process subscription payments via PayU
3. Data Storage and Security
Your exam responses are stored locally on your device using an encrypted SQLite database.
Cloud data is stored on Google Firebase (Firestore), hosted in Google Cloud data centers.
We use industry-standard encryption for data in transit (TLS) and at rest.
We do not store question text or images from exam papers (only your answer selections and answer key data).
4. Data Sharing
We do not sell your personal data. We share data only with:

Firebase (Google): For authentication, cloud storage, analytics, and crash reporting.
Google AdMob: For serving advertisements to free tier users.
PayU: For processing subscription payments. PayU receives your name, email, and phone number for payment processing only.
5. Your Rights
Access: You can view all your data within the App (Profile → Attempt History).
Deletion: You can delete your account and all associated data by contacting us at privacy@examkey.app.
Opt-out of notifications: You can disable notifications in the App settings or your device settings.
Ad personalization: You can opt out of personalized ads through your device's ad settings.
6. Guest Mode
You can use ExamKey without creating an account. In guest mode, your data is stored only on your device and is not synced to our servers. Guest data cannot be recovered if you uninstall the App.

7. Children's Privacy
ExamKey is designed for students aged 16 and above preparing for competitive exams. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If you believe we have collected data from a child under 16, please contact us at privacy@examkey.app.

8. Data Retention
User attempts and scores: Retained indefinitely while your account is active.
Answer key data: Retained for up to 5 years.
Analytics data: Retained for 14 months (Firebase default).
9. Compliance
This policy is designed to comply with:

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), 2023
Google Play Developer Policy
AdMob policies
10. Changes to This Policy
We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes through the App or via push notification.
